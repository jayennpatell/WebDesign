Get Involved

You don’t need a psychology degree to make a difference. Here’s how you can support mental health in your community — and beyond.

Ways to Take Action

  • Start conversations: Talking openly about mental health reduces stigma.
  • Share resources: A simple post can lead someone to the help they need.
  • Volunteer your time: Local support groups, school clubs, and awareness events always need help.
  • Create safe spaces: Let people know you're someone they can talk to without judgment.
  • Be an advocate: Write to local leaders or participate in mental health policy events.

Join a Campaign

Campaigns help amplify your voice and reach thousands who may need support or education. Whether you walk, post, fundraise, or just wear a pin — it matters.

  • Bell Let’s Talk: Every January, this campaign donates based on shared messages of support.
  • Mental Health Awareness Month: May is filled with global initiatives, stories, and school events.
  • Jack.org: A youth-led Canadian movement changing how we think about mental health.
  • World Suicide Prevention Day: September 10th — host or attend events in your area.

Resources & Hotlines

If you or someone you know needs immediate support, reach out to the organizations below. These professionals are trained to listen, guide, and help — no matter what you're going through.

  • Talk Suicide Canada: 1-833-456-4566 (24/7)
  • Kids Help Phone: Text CONNECT to 686868 or call 1-800-668-6868
  • NAMI (US): 1-800-950-NAMI (Mon–Fri)
  • International Resources: Visit findahelpline.com

Small Actions, Big Impact

You don’t have to do everything. Just do something. Even small acts — checking in on a friend, sharing a hotline, posting a mental health tip — can have a ripple effect. The more we normalize these conversations, the more lives we impact.

Remember: change starts with awareness, and awareness starts with you.

Join the Movement

Want to make a long-term impact? Join or start a mental health club at your school. Attend local awareness walks. Speak up when someone needs help.
